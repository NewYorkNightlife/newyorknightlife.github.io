Weekend Overview

Month-end winter weekends can outperform expectations when routed correctly. The common mistake is chasing “bigger” options late. The better approach is compact neighborhood sequencing and clear stop-loss rules for lines and transfers.

What Changed This Week

This week is a transition weekend. Compared with mid-February, demand quality is more uneven venue-to-venue. Good nights come from fast filtering and compact movement—not from trying to cover too much city.

Night Route Strategies

Run two-stop architecture: one anchor + one quality pivot. Avoid unnecessary third-stop greed unless both timing and transport are favorable.

Budget + Risk Strategy

Cold lows increase indoor bottlenecks. Set queue thresholds and enforce them fast. Budget movement explicitly so surge doesn’t become your largest line item.
